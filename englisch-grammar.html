Tenses — Zeitformen

Alle wichtigen Zeiten für die ZP 10

Für die ZP brauchst du vor allem: Simple Past (Reading), Present Perfect (Reading), Will-Future und Simple Present (Writing). (для ЦП самые важные: Simple Past, Present Perfect, Will-Future и Simple Present)

📋 Übersicht aller Zeitformen

ZeitformBildungSignalwörterBeispiel
Simple PresentI play / he playsalways, every day, usually, oftenShe plays tennis every day.
Present Progressiveam/is/are + -ingnow, at the moment, right nowI am playing tennis now.
Simple Pastplayed / went (2nd form)yesterday, last week, ago, in 2020She played tennis yesterday.
Past Progressivewas/were + -ingwhile, when (Hintergrund)I was playing when he called.
Present Perfecthave/has + past participlealready, yet, ever, never, since, for, justShe has played tennis since 2019.
Past Perfecthad + past participleafter, before (Vorvergangenheit)After she had played, she went home.
Will-Futurewill + Infinitivtomorrow, next week, I think, probablyI will play tennis tomorrow.
Going-to-Futuream/is/are going to + InfinitivPlan, Absicht, VorhabenI am going to play tennis later.

🟢 Simple Present (простое настоящее)

Bildung: Infinitiv (bei he/she/it + -s)

FormBeispiel
✅ PositivI play football. / He plays football.
❌ NegativI do not (don’t) play football. / He does not (doesn’t) play football.
❓ FrageDo you play football? / Does he play football?

Signalwörter: always every day usually often sometimes never

Beispiele:

  • She always drinks coffee in the morning.
  • They go to school every day.

🟡 Present Progressive (настоящее длительное)

Bildung: am / is / are + Verb-ing

FormBeispiel
✅ PositivI am reading a book. / She is reading a book.
❌ NegativI am not reading. / She is not (isn’t) reading.
❓ FrageAre you reading? / Is she reading?

Signalwörter: now at the moment right now Look! Listen!

Beispiele:

  • Look! The cat is sleeping on the sofa.
  • We are studying English at the moment.

🔴 Simple Past (простое прошедшее)

Bildung: regelmäßig: Verb + -ed — unregelmäßig: 2. Form (go → went, see → saw)

FormBeispiel
✅ PositivI played tennis. / I went to school.
❌ NegativI did not (didn’t) play tennis. / I didn’t go to school.
❓ FrageDid you play tennis? / Did you go to school?

Signalwörter: yesterday last week/month/year ago in 2020

Beispiele:

  • We visited London last summer.
  • She bought a new phone two days ago.

🟠 Past Progressive (прошедшее длительное)

Bildung: was / were + Verb-ing

FormBeispiel
✅ PositivI was watching TV. / They were playing outside.
❌ NegativI was not (wasn’t) watching TV.
❓ FrageWere you watching TV?

Signalwörter: while when (Hintergrundhandlung)

Verwendung: Hintergrundhandlung, die durch ein anderes Ereignis unterbrochen wird.

  • I was sleeping when the phone rang.
  • While she was cooking, he was reading.

🟣 Present Perfect (настоящее совершённое)

Bildung: have / has + Past Participle (3. Form)

FormBeispiel
✅ PositivI have played tennis. / She has visited London.
❌ NegativI have not (haven’t) played tennis.
❓ FrageHave you ever played tennis?

Signalwörter: already yet ever never just since for

Beispiele:

  • I have already finished my homework.
  • She has lived here since 2018.

🔵 Past Perfect (предпрошедшее время)

Bildung: had + Past Participle (3. Form)

FormBeispiel
✅ PositivI had finished my work before she arrived.
❌ NegativI had not (hadn’t) finished my work.
❓ FrageHad you finished your work?

Signalwörter: after before already when (Vorvergangenheit — предпрошедшее)

Beispiele:

  • After I had eaten, I went for a walk.
  • She had already left before I arrived.

🟢 Will-Future (будущее с will)

Bildung: will + Infinitiv

FormBeispiel
✅ PositivI will play tennis tomorrow.
❌ NegativI will not (won’t) play tennis.
❓ FrageWill you play tennis?

Signalwörter: tomorrow next week I think probably perhaps

Verwendung: Spontane Entscheidungen, Vorhersagen, Vermutungen, Versprechen.

  • I think it will rain tomorrow.
  • I will help you with your homework.

🟡 Going-to-Future (будущее с going to)

Bildung: am / is / are + going to + Infinitiv

FormBeispiel
✅ PositivI am going to visit my grandma.
❌ NegativI am not going to visit my grandma.
❓ FrageAre you going to visit your grandma?

Verwendung: Geplante Handlungen, feste Absichten (запланированные действия)

  • We are going to travel to Spain this summer.
  • She is going to study medicine.

If-Clauses — Bedingungssätze

Drei Typen — real, irreal, unmöglich (условные предложения)

Merke: Der If-Satz beschreibt die Bedingung, der Hauptsatz die Folge. Im If-Satz steht niemals “will”! (в if-части никогда не ставим will!)

⚪ Type 0 — Zero Conditional (нулевое условие — общие истины)

Bildung: If + Simple Present, ... Simple Present

Verwendung: Allgemeine Wahrheiten, Naturgesetze, Gewohnheiten — Dinge, die immer passieren, wenn die Bedingung erfüllt ist. (всегда верно — 100%, не «возможно».)

If you heat water to 100°C, it boils.

If it rains, the ground gets wet.

If you mix red and blue, you get purple.

If I am tired, I go to bed early. (привычка)

Tipp: Statt «if» kannst du oft «when» einsetzen, ohne die Bedeutung zu ändern — When you heat water, it boils. Bei Type 1 geht das nicht!

Unterschied zu Type 1: Type 0 = immer wahr (Gesetz). Type 1 = einmal in der Zukunft möglich.

🟢 Type 1 — Real / Möglich (реальное условие)

Bildung: If + Simple Present, ... will + Infinitiv

Verwendung: Etwas ist realistisch und kann tatsächlich passieren.

If it rains, I will stay home.

If you study hard, you will pass the exam.

If she calls me, I will answer.

🟡 Type 2 — Irreal / Hypothetisch (нереальное условие — настоящее)

Bildung: If + Simple Past, ... would + Infinitiv

Verwendung: Etwas ist im Moment unwahrscheinlich oder nicht real.

If I had money, I would buy a car.

If I were you, I would study more.

If she lived in London, she would speak better English.

Hinweis: Bei “to be” im If-Satz benutzt man were für alle Personen (If I were...).

🔴 Type 3 — Unmöglich / Vergangenheit (нереальное условие — прошлое)

Bildung: If + Past Perfect, ... would have + Past Participle

Verwendung: Etwas ist in der Vergangenheit nicht passiert — man kann es nicht mehr ändern.

If I had studied, I would have passed the exam.

If she had called me, I would have helped her.

If we had left earlier, we would have caught the train.

📋 Vergleich — Alle 3 Typen

TypIf-SatzHauptsatzBedeutung
Type 0If + Simple PresentSimple Presentimmer wahr (Gesetz, Gewohnheit)
Type 1If + Simple Presentwill + Infinitivrealistisch, möglich
Type 2If + Simple Pastwould + Infinitivhypothetisch, irreal
Type 3If + Past Perfectwould have + Past Participleunmöglich (Vergangenheit)

⚠️ Häufige Fehler (типичные ошибки)

FalschRichtigErklärung
If I will go...If I go...Kein “will” im If-Satz!
If I would have...If I had...Type 2: Simple Past, nicht would
If I would have had...If I had had...Type 3: Past Perfect, nicht would have

✍️ Übungen — Welcher Typ?

0. If you _____ (not water) plants, they _____ (die).

Lösung anzeigen

Type 0: If you don’t water plants, they die. (Allgemeine Wahrheit — immer so.)

1. If she _____ (study) harder, she _____ (get) better grades.

Lösung anzeigen

Type 1: If she studies harder, she will get better grades. (Es ist realistisch möglich.)

2. If I _____ (be) rich, I _____ (travel) the world.

Lösung anzeigen

Type 2: If I were rich, I would travel the world. (Hypothetisch — ich bin nicht reich.)

3. If they _____ (leave) earlier, they _____ (not miss) the bus.

Lösung anzeigen

Type 3: If they had left earlier, they would not have missed the bus. (Vergangenheit — nicht mehr änderbar.)

4. If it _____ (rain) tomorrow, we _____ (stay) at home.

Lösung anzeigen

Type 1: If it rains tomorrow, we will stay at home. (Möglich — es könnte regnen.)

Modal Verbs — Modalverben

must / should / can / could / might / have to (модальные глаголы)

Modalverben verändern die Bedeutung des Hauptverbs: können, müssen, sollen, dürfen, mögen. Nach einem Modalverb steht der Infinitiv ohne «to»: I can swim, NICHT I can to swim.

📋 Übersicht — alle Modalverben

ModalDeutschBedeutungBeispiel
cankönnenFähigkeit / MöglichkeitI can speak English.
couldkonnte / könnteVergangenheit / höfliche BitteShe could read at 4. / Could you help me?
mustmüsseninnere VerpflichtungI must study harder.
have tomüssen (Pflicht)äußere Regel / PflichtYou have to wear a uniform.
shouldsollen / sollteRat / EmpfehlungYou should drink more water.
maydürfen / vielleichtErlaubnis (höflich) / MöglichkeitIt may rain. / May I come in?
mightkönnte (vielleicht)geringere WahrscheinlichkeitShe might come later.
willwerdenZukunft / EntscheidungI will call you tomorrow.
wouldwürdeKonjunktiv / höflichI would like some tea.
shallsollen (Vorschlag)Vorschlag (1. Person)Shall we go?

⚡ 4 wichtige Regeln

  1. Nach dem Modalverb — Infinitiv ohne to: I can swim (NICHT I can to swim)
  2. Modalverben haben kein -s in der 3. Person Singular: He can (NICHT He cans)
  3. Frage: Modalverb steht am Anfang: Can you help? (NICHT Do you can help?)
  4. Verneinung: Modalverb + not: I cannot / I can’t / I shouldn’t

⚖️ must vs. have to — der Unterschied

musthave to
Woher kommt die Pflicht?von innen (eigene Entscheidung)von außen (Regel, Vorschrift)
BeispielI must learn more. (ich will selbst)I have to wear a tie at school. (Schulregel)
Vergangenheit❌ keine FormI had to stay home.
Zukunft❌ keine FormI will have to work hard.

💡 In Vergangenheit und Zukunft nimm immer have to.

⚠️ mustn’t ≠ don’t have to — GEFAHR

FormBedeutungBeispiel
mustn’tVERBOT (man darf NICHT)You mustn’t smoke here. (Verbot)
don’t have tokeine Pflicht (man muss nicht, kann aber)You don’t have to come. (du kannst kommen, musst aber nicht)

Wenn du das verwechselst — sagst du das Gegenteil! Merke dir: mustn’t = VERBOT.

🤔 Vermutungen — wie sicher bist du?

Von «100% sicher» bis «vielleicht»:

Modal%Beispiel
must~95% sicherShe must be tired. (sie muss müde sein)
may~50%She may be at home. (es ist gut möglich)
might~30%She might come, but not sure. (eher unsicher)
could~30%It could rain later.
can’t~95% sicher NICHTIt can’t be true! (unmöglich)

🎯 Für die ZP — das musst du können

  • can / could — Fähigkeit und Höflichkeit
  • must / have to — Verpflichtung
  • should — Rat / Empfehlung (oft im Writing!): You should learn more.
  • would like — höfliche Bitte: I would like to apply for…
  • might / may — für Comment: This might be the reason why…

Passive — Passiv

Aktiv vs. Passiv — Wann und wie? (пассивный залог)

Grundregel: Passiv = to be (in der passenden Zeit) + Past Participle (3. Form). Man benutzt Passiv, wenn die Handlung wichtiger ist als der Täter. (пассив = to be + 3-я форма глагола)

🔄 Aktiv vs. Passiv

Aktiv➡Passiv
The dog bites the man.➡The man is bitten by the dog.
She wrote the letter.➡The letter was written by her.

Das Objekt des Aktivsatzes wird zum Subjekt des Passivsatzes. (объект активного → подлежащее пассивного)

🔧 Bildung: to be + Past Participle

1
Objekt nach vorne

Das Objekt des Aktivsatzes wird zum Subjekt.

2
“to be” in der richtigen Zeit

Simple Present → is/am/are — Simple Past → was/were usw.

3
Past Participle (3. Form)

done, written, spoken, played, seen usw.

4
“by” + Täter (optional)

Nur erwähnen, wenn der Täter wichtig ist.

📋 Passiv in verschiedenen Zeiten

ZeitformAktivPassiv
Simple PresentShe writes a letter.A letter is written.
Simple PastShe wrote a letter.A letter was written.
Present PerfectShe has written a letter.A letter has been written.
Will-FutureShe will write a letter.A letter will be written.
ModalsShe can write a letter.A letter can be written.

❓ Wann benutzt man Passiv?

  • Der Täter ist unbekannt: My bike was stolen.
  • Der Täter ist unwichtig: English is spoken all over the world.
  • Die Handlung steht im Fokus: The bridge was built in 1900.
  • In formellen Texten: The results were published last week.

👤 “by” — Den Täter erwähnen

Wenn der Täter wichtig oder neu ist, benutzt man by:

  • The book was written by J.K. Rowling.
  • The cake was made by my grandmother.

Wenn der Täter unwichtig ist, lässt man “by” weg.

✍️ Übungen — Aktiv ➡ Passiv

1. Somebody cleans the room every day. ➡ Passiv?

Lösung anzeigen

The room is cleaned every day.

2. They built the house in 1990. ➡ Passiv?

Lösung anzeigen

The house was built in 1990.

3. She has already sent the email. ➡ Passiv?

Lösung anzeigen

The email has already been sent.

4. They will open the new shop next week. ➡ Passiv?

Lösung anzeigen

The new shop will be opened next week.

Comparatives & Superlatives

Steigerung der Adjektive (сравнительная и превосходная степень)

3 Steigerungsstufen: big (Positiv) → bigger (Komparativ) → the biggest (Superlativ). Die Regeln hängen von der Anzahl der Silben ab.

📐 Hauptregel: Silben zählen

Adjektiv-TypKomparativSuperlativBeispiel
1 Silbe (z.B. tall)+ erthe + esttall → taller → the tallest
1 Silbe + e (nice)+ rthe + stnice → nicer → the nicest
1 Silbe + Konsonant (big, hot)Konsonant verdoppeln + erthe + verdoppeln + estbig → bigger → the biggest
2 Silben + y (happy)y → i + erthe + i + esthappy → happier → the happiest
2+ Silben (interesting)more + Adjektivthe most + Adjektivinteresting → more interesting → the most interesting

💡 Silbe = единица произношения, ein Vokal-Klang. tall = 1 Silbe (тал). happy = 2 Silben (хэп-пи). interesting = 4 Silben (ин-те-рес-тинг).

⚠️ Unregelmäßige Steigerung — AUSWENDIG LERNEN

PositivKomparativSuperlativ
good gutbetterthe best
bad schlechtworsethe worst
far weit / fernfarther / furtherthe farthest / furthest
little weniglessthe least
much / many vielmorethe most
old alt (bei Familie)elderthe eldest

📝 Vergleichsstrukturen

StrukturBedeutungBeispiel
as … asgenauso… wie (Gleichheit)She is as tall as me.
not as … asnicht so… wieHe is not as fast as her.
-er than / more … thanKomparativ + alsLondon is bigger than Berlin.
less … thanweniger … alsThis task is less difficult than the last.
the -est / the most …Superlativ (am …sten)It is the most interesting book.
the …er, the …erje… desto…The more you learn, the better you get.

⚠️ Typische Fehler

❌ FALSCH✅ RICHTIGGrund

more betterbetterbetter ist schon Komparativ
the most biggestthe biggestnicht doppelt steigern
more interesting thatmore interesting thanthan = als (Vergleich)

more happyhappier2 Silben + y → happier

🎯 Für Writing

  • Comment / Article: This is more important than… / One of the biggest problems is…
  • Story: It was the best day of my life. / The food was better than I expected.
  • Email: I would prefer the earlier date. / This option is cheaper.

Relative Clauses — Relativsätze

Sätze mit who, which, that, whose, where (относительные предложения)

Relativsätze geben zusätzliche Informationen über ein Nomen. Sie werden mit Relativpronomen eingeleitet. (относительные предложения дают доп. информацию о существительном)

📋 Relativpronomen

PronomenBezieht sich aufBeispiel
whoPersonenThe boy who lives next door is my friend.
whichSachen / TiereThe book which I read was great.
thatPersonen und SachenThe film that we watched was exciting.
whoseBesitz (wessen)The girl whose bag was stolen called the police.
whereOrteThe city where I grew up is beautiful.

✅ Defining vs. 📌 Non-defining Relative Clauses

Defining (notwendig)Non-defining (zusätzlich)
Komma?NeinJa — mit Komma
that?Ja, “that” möglichNein — kein “that”
Weglassbar?Nein — Satz unverständlichJa — Satz bleibt verständlich
BeispielThe boy who lives next door is nice.London, which is the capital, is big.

💬 Contact Clauses (Kontaktsätze)

In Defining Relative Clauses kann man das Pronomen weglassen, wenn es Objekt ist:

  • The film (that) I saw yesterday was great. → The film I saw yesterday was great.
  • The girl (who/that) I met was friendly. → The girl I met was friendly.

Wenn das Pronomen Subjekt ist, kann man es nicht weglassen.

Reported Speech — Indirekte Rede

Direkte Rede ➡ Indirekte Rede (косвенная речь)

Grundregel: Bei Reported Speech verschiebt sich die Zeit um eine Stufe zurück (Backshift). (время сдвигается на одну ступень назад)

🔄 Backshift of Tenses — Zeitverschiebung

Direct Speech➡Reported Speech
“I am happy.”➡He said (that) he was happy.
“I play football.”➡He said he played football.
“I am playing.”➡He said he was playing.
“I played football.”➡He said he had played football.
“I have visited London.”➡He said he had visited London.
“I will come.”➡He said he would come.
“I can swim.”➡He said he could swim.
“I must go.”➡He said he had to go.

📍 Zeit- und Ortsangaben ändern sich

Direct➡Reported
today➡that day
tomorrow➡the next day / the following day
yesterday➡the day before / the previous day
here➡there
this➡that
now➡then / at that time
ago➡before / earlier

🗣️ Say vs. Tell

VerbVerwendungBeispiel
saysay + something (ohne Person)He said that he was tired.
telltell + someone + somethingHe told me that he was tired.

Merke: say = etwas sagen — tell = jemandem etwas sagen/erzählen.

✍️ Übungen — Reported Speech

1. “I am going to the cinema.” ➡ She said...

Lösung anzeigen

She said (that) she was going to the cinema.

2. “I will help you tomorrow.” ➡ He told me...

Lösung anzeigen

He told me (that) he would help me the next day.

3. “I have never been to Paris.” ➡ She said...

Lösung anzeigen

She said (that) she had never been to Paris.

4. “I can speak French.” ➡ He said...

Lösung anzeigen

He said (that) he could speak French.

Useful Structures — Nützliche Strukturen

Grammatik-Muster für Writing und Sprachgebrauch

Diese Strukturen helfen dir, bessere Sätze im Writing-Teil zu schreiben und typische Fehler zu vermeiden. (эти структуры помогут писать лучше в части Writing)

📊 Vergleiche — Comparisons (сравнения)

StrukturVerwendungBeispiel
as ... asGleichheitShe is as tall as her brother.
not as ... asUngleichheitHe is not as fast as she is.
-er ... than / more ... thanKomparativLondon is bigger than Berlin. / English is more important than French.
the -est / the mostSuperlativShe is the tallest girl. / It is the most interesting book.
less ... thanweniger alsThis task is less difficult than the last one.

Regel: Kurze Adjektive (1-2 Silben): -er / -est. Lange Adjektive (3+ Silben): more / most. Ausnahmen: good → better → best, bad → worse → worst.

📝 Gerund vs. Infinitive (герундий или инфинитив)

Gerund (-ing)Infinitive (to + Verb)
enjoy doingwant to do
stop doingdecide to do
finish doinghope to do
mind doingplan to do
avoid doingagree to do
suggest doinglearn to do

Beispiele:

  • I enjoy reading books.
  • She wants to travel to Spain.
  • They stopped talking when the teacher came in.

❓ Question Tags (разделительные вопросы)

Regel: Positiver Satz ➡ negativer Tag. Negativer Satz ➡ positiver Tag.

SatzQuestion Tag

She can swim,can’t she?
He is coming,isn’t he?

💡 Some / Any / No (some / any / no)

WortVerwendungBeispiel
somePositive Sätze, Angebote, BittenI have some friends. / Would you like some tea?
anyNegative Sätze, FragenI don’t have any money. / Do you have any questions?
no= not any (Verneinung ohne “not”)I have no money. (= I don’t have any money.)

Gleiches Prinzip für: something/anything/nothing, someone/anyone/no one, somewhere/anywhere/nowhere.

🔢 Much / Many / A lot of (много)

WortVerwendungBeispiel
muchUnzählbar (несчитаемое) — Fragen / VerneinungenHow much water? / I don’t have much time.
manyZählbar (считаемое) — Fragen / VerneinungenHow many books? / There aren’t many people.
a lot ofBeides — positive SätzeShe has a lot of friends. / He drinks a lot of coffee.

⏳ Since vs. For (с каких пор / как долго)

WortVerwendungBeispiel
sinceZeitpunkt (с какого момента)I have lived here since 2019. / since Monday / since last summer
forZeitraum (как долго)I have lived here for 5 years. / for two weeks / for a long time

Merke: since + Zeitpunkt (2019, Monday, January) — for + Zeitraum (5 years, 3 hours, a long time).
