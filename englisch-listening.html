Listening Strategies

What to do before, during and after listening

📋 Before listening (до прослушивания)

1
Read the questions first!

Read ALL questions BEFORE the audio starts — know what to listen for.

2
Underline key words

Mark the important words in each question: who? what? where? when? why?

3
Predict possible answers

Try to guess what the answers might be — this activates your brain and helps you focus.

4
Have a pen ready

You can make notes on the question sheet while listening. Short notes, not full sentences!

👂 While listening (во время прослушивания)

You will hear the text TWICE. First time: get the main idea. Second time: check details and fill gaps.

1
Take short notes

Write key words — numbers, names, places. NOT full sentences!

2
Don't panic if you miss a word

Keep listening! The context will usually help you understand. Never stop — you'll miss more.

3
Listen for signal words

Words like however, therefore, although, in addition show the structure and highlight key points.

4
Notice stress & intonation

Stressed words are the most important ones. If the speaker's voice rises — that's a key moment.

5
Watch for synonyms

The question might say happy but the speaker says pleased. The meaning is the same — don't miss it!

✏️ After listening (после прослушивания)

1
Complete your answers

Fill in your notes while the information is still fresh. Use the second listening to check and add details.

2
Write full sentences if asked

Write complete sentences for short-answer questions. Don't leave anything blank — a partial answer is better than nothing!

3
Never leave a question empty

Even if you're not sure — always write something. An empty answer gets zero points!

Task Types

Different formats — different strategies

In the ZP 10 listening exam you get several audio texts (e.g. news, interviews, announcements, conversations). Each comes with tasks in different formats:

Multiple Choice Gap Filling Matching True / False / Not in the text

✅ 1. Multiple Choice

You get a question with 3 options (A, B, C). Pick ONE correct answer.

Strategy:

  • Read ALL options BEFORE the audio plays
  • Cross out obviously wrong answers — raises your chance
  • Watch out for similar-sounding options — one detail makes the difference
  • Questions follow the order of the audio — don't get stuck on one
  • Unsure? Mark it lightly and check on the second listening

✍️ 2. Gap Filling

Write the missing word(s) in the gaps. Usually numbers, names, places or key terms.

Strategy:

  • Read the sentence around the gap — what type of word is missing? (noun? number? adjective?)
  • Write exactly what you hear — the word is usually taken directly from the audio
  • Numbers and names are the easiest — listen for them carefully
  • After listening, check grammar: does the word fit the sentence?

🔗 3. Matching

Match speakers to statements or topics. E.g. who said what? who has which opinion?

Strategy:

  • Read all the statements (List A) before the audio — there are more options than speakers
  • Listen carefully when a new speaker begins — each speaker gets their own match
  • Not all options will be used — don't force a match if it doesn't fit
  • Watch for synonyms — a speaker says happy, the list says pleased

❓ 4. True / False / Not in the text

Decide: is the statement true, false, or not mentioned at all?

Strategy:

  • True = exactly what the speaker said (or a paraphrase)
  • False = the speaker said the OPPOSITE
  • Not in the text = the topic was never mentioned at all
  • Only choose "not in the text" if you're really sure — not just because you forgot!
  • Watch for negatives: not, never, hardly can flip the meaning

📢 Typical audio types & topics

Radio programmes Interviews News reports Announcements Conversations / dialogues Podcasts Phone calls

Speakers may have British or American accents. Topics include: school & work, environment, media & technology, travel, friendship, human rights.
